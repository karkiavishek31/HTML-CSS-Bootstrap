Popular Destinations

Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) is a popular trekking destination in the Annapurna region of Nepal. The trek takes around 7-12 days and is known for its beautiful scenery, ethnic villages, and stunning mountain views. The trail takes trekkers through rhododendron forests, rivers, and traditional Gurung communities. The highlight of the trek is reaching the base camp of the 10th highest mountain in the world, Annapurna (8091m). The base camp offers panoramic views of several Himalayan peaks including, Annapurna South, Machapuchare (6993m), and Hiunchuli (6441m). The trek is considered moderate in terms of difficulty and altitude sickness risks, making it accessible for most trekkers. The best time to trek to Annapurna Base Camp is between the months of October to November and March to May as the weather is usually clear, dry, and perfect for trekking

Mustang is a region located in the northern-central part of Nepal. It is a part of the Mustang District, situated in the western part of the country. The region is rich in cultural, natural, and historical significance, making it a popular tourist destination in Nepal. The area is known for its unique landscapes, including the scenic Kali Gandaki River Gorge and the Annapurna mountain range. It is also home to several ancient monasteries and caves, such as the renowned Luri Gompa and the Kagbeni Caves. The diverse population in Lower Mustang is made up of ethnic groups like Thakalis, Gurungs, and Tibetans, who have their unique traditions, festivals, and cuisines. The region's economy mainly depends on tourism, agriculture, and commerce. The best time to visit Lower Mustang is during the autumn and spring seasons, when the weather is pleasant, and the region's natural beauty is at its peak.

Pokhara Valley is a popular tourist destination located in central Nepal. It is approximately 200 kilometers west of the capital city, Kathmandu. The valley is known for its natural beauty, including stunning views of snow-capped mountains and clear, tranquil lakes. The most famous lake in Pokhara is Phewa Lake, which attracts many visitors who enjoy boating or just soaking up the picturesque scenery. The valley is also home to a number of adventure sports such as paragliding, zip-lining, and trekking. In addition, there are several Buddhist and Hindu temples and monasteries that are worth a visit. The relaxed atmosphere of Pokhara Valley, combined with its spectacular natural setting and vibrant culture, make it a must-visit destination for travelers to Nepal.
